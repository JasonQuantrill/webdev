Crazy Jacksonville Facts

Jacksonville has more than 840 square miles

Jacksonville, Florida, holds the distinction of being the largest city by land area in the contiguous United States, covering over 840 square miles. This extensive territory encompasses a diverse range of urban neighborhoods, natural reserves, and recreational opportunities, making Jacksonville a city of vast exploration.

There are on average 270 days of sunshine per year

Jacksonville, Florida is often referred to as the "Sunshine City" with an annual average of 270 days of sunshine, creating an ideal environment for outdoor activities, including beach outings, golfing, and vibrant community events. This sunny climate is a defining feature of Jacksonville, adding to its charm as a city that celebrates the great outdoors.

The military employs over 30,000 active-duty personnel and nearly 20,000 civilians in the Jacksonville area and is the largest employer in the county

Jacksonville, Florida, is home to a significant military presence, with over 30,000 active-duty personnel and nearly 20,000 civilians employed in the region, making the military the largest employer in the county. This strong military presence not only contributes to the area's economy but also fosters a deep connection between the city and the armed forces, reflecting Jacksonville's pivotal role in supporting the nation's defense.

Attractions

Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens

The Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens is a captivating destination where wildlife and botanical wonders come together to offer a unique and immersive experience. With over 2,000 animals representing a wide range of species and beautifully landscaped gardens, it's a place that both nature enthusiasts and families can enjoy.

The Cummer Museum of Art and Gardens

The Cummer Museum of Art and Gardens is a cultural gem, offering a captivating blend of world-class art collections and stunning formal gardens. Visitors can explore a rich tapestry of artistic treasures and immerse themselves in the serene beauty of the historic gardens, making it a delightful destination for art and nature lovers alike.

Catty Shack Ranch

The Catty Shack Ranch Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for big cat lovers and a dedicated refuge for rescued exotic cats, including tigers, lions, leopards, and cougars. This nonprofit sanctuary provides a unique opportunity to learn about these majestic creatures while supporting their welfare and conservation.

Yummy Food

The Potter's House Soul Food Bistro

The Potter's House Soul Food Bistro serves up a delicious taste of Southern comfort food with a welcoming and community-oriented atmosphere. From savory fried chicken to mouthwatering collard greens, this restaurant is a must-visit for those looking to savor traditional soul food favorites in a friendly setting.

Bones Pizza

Bones Pizza is a local culinary gem, known for its delectable, handcrafted pizzas that boast a perfect blend of flavors and high-quality ingredients. Whether you're a fan of classic Margherita or crave unique creations, Bones Pizza is the go-to spot for pizza enthusiasts seeking a slice of perfection.

Jenkins Quality Barbecue

Jenkins Quality Barbecue is celebrated for its mouthwatering, slow-cooked barbecue dishes that embody the essence of Southern cuisine. Whether you're savoring tender pulled pork or sipping their renowned sauce, a visit to Jenkins is a flavorful journey into the heart of barbecue tradition.
